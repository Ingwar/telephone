There is a children's game called telephone in which a queue of people pass a message from ear to ear to the end of the line. The message that comes out the other end rarely resembles the original message, even if each step of the way the message only departs from the previous by a little bit.

We are researchers at University of Wisconsin-Madison who are interested in documenting the process of auditory transmission from person to person in a game much like telephone. If you choose to participate in our study, we will ask you to play the telephone game with others: you will listen to an audio message and then record yourself recreating as closely as possible the message you heard.

The interface below is used to play the game. Clicking on the top sound wave icon will play a sound, and clicking on the bottom sound wave will toggle your computer's microphone recorder so that you can record your own. Our game differs from the children's game in that many of the sounds you will hear will not be recognizable as English words. Your task is the same: to recreate the sound you heard as accurately as you can.

If you choose to participate in our game, you will be asked to listen to and record audio using your internet browser. Unfortunately we only know how to do that with Mozilla Firefox or Google Chrome browsers. Please visit the site using either of these browsers or you will not be able to participate.

You must give your browser explicit permission before it can make a recording by clicking on the "Share Mic" button in the nav bar on the next page. You won't be able to listen to the message until you have shared your microphone. After you have listened to the message, you will be allowed to make your own recording. Click the bottom sound wave to turn the recorder on, and click a second time to turn the recorder off.

After you have made a recording, press Submit to send your entry to us. The next person who arrives at the site will now hear your recording, and record their own recreation of your recreation (and so on, and so on!). Don't worry, we will not ask you to provide any individuating information in your recordings. Each player will be asked to recreate a few messages before completion.

When you are ready to begin, click Accept below.
